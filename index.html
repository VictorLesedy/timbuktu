Our story

The city where
the world met

Centuries ago, Timbuktu stood at the crossroads of the world. Merchants crossed deserts. Scholars crossed continents. Artists shared stories — and history was written because remarkable people gathered in one place.

That spirit guides everything we do. Every generation deserves its own crossroads: a place to meet, connect, celebrate, and make memories that last long after the music ends. We exist to create those moments — not just events, but nights worth remembering.

Good to know

Questions,
answered

The things people DM us about most. Anything else — reach out on WhatsApp.

All events are strictly 18+ — bring a valid ID matching your ticket. Have your QR ticket ready on your phone (screenshots are fine). Some nights have a dress code; check your event card.

Tickets are sold in Tanzanian Shillings. At checkout you'll be able to pay by mobile money (M-Pesa, Tigo Pesa, Airtel Money, HaloPesa) or card. You'll receive your QR ticket by SMS and email instantly.

Every It's A Cold Summer ticket includes a return boat transfer to Bongoyo Island with a welcome drink on board. Boats run on a published schedule from a Dar pick-up point — last return is after the closing set. You must travel on a Timbuktu boat; private boats aren't permitted for the buyout.

Yes — it's part of the experience. "No hat, no entry" at Wild Wild East, all-white or icy blue at Cold Summer, corporate-but-sexy at Suit & Tie. Guests who don't meet the code may be turned away without a refund, so dress the part.

Tickets are non-refundable but fully transferable up to 24 hours before doors — just pass on your QR or re-assign it from your confirmation. If an event is rescheduled, your ticket stays valid for the new date or you can request a refund.

One pass covers all four 2026 nights at a discount versus buying separately, in Standard, VIP, or VVIP tiers. Attend all four and you earn a closing perk at the Suit & Tie finale. Passes are limited and transfer the same way as single tickets.
